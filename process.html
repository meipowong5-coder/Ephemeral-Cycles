GT Process Documentation: Ephemeral Cycles

08/2025 – Initial Brainstorming
Explored memento mori as core concept.
First idea: clock with barrier-grid animation.
Goal: gentle reminder of life’s transience via nature visuals.
09/2025 – First Advisor Meeting
Discussed initial idea.
Advisor suggested more research on “what is time?”
Key questions: What defines time? How to interpret it differently?
09/2025 – Research Phase
Memento Mori: Latin for “remember you must die”. Used in art for humility and mindfulness. Examples: vanitas paintings, skulls (avoiding dark themes).
Time Perspectives (3 Categories):
Physical: Mechanical, like clocks and seconds.
Cosmological: Cyclical, like sun and seasons.
Philosophical: Subjective, like memory and presence.
10/2025 – Personal Reflection
Felt time accelerate after secondary school.
Struggle to "keep up" with time despite hard work.
Question: What does cherishing time mean?
Conclusion: Ignore time; focus on the present moment.
10/2025 – Project Expansion
Expanded beyond one clock.
One artwork per time perspective.
Physical: Barrier-grid clock.
Cosmological: Sundial with shadows.
Philosophical: Non-functional timepiece for presence.
Outcome: Series of transformed time devices as gentle memento mori.
10/2025 – Technical Work
Focus: Barrier-grid animation for clock.
Challenge: Shorten videos, reduce stripe width for clarity, interlace for circle.
Status: Testing code and prints.
Decision: Use original content to avoid copyright.
Animation Plan:
Outer: Acorn to tree decay or blossom to rot.
Middle: Strawberry to shrivel.
Inner: Daylily or morning glory bloom.
11/2025 – Technical Development & Animation Logic
Goal: Reduce stripe width for brightness.
Extracted frames, created interlaced strips.
Working on circular stitching in Processing.
Method:
1. Video (518 frames) split into 129 groups (4 frames each).
2. Interlace each group (5px slit).
3. Arrange in donut ring for 516 frames.
4. Stripe width ~1.8mm on 30cm clock.
Insight: Same sequence, vary speed for pace.
Strategy: 24-frame animation for all rings. Metaphor: One life on different scales.
11/2025 – Technical Development
Achievement: Created three-ring overlay in Illustrator with aligned grids.
Goal: Match interlaced images to overlay using Illustrator.
11/2025 – Project Contraction
Abandoned series for one unified installation.
Physical: Rotating cylinder with plant animation.
Cosmological: Light for shifting shadows.
Philosophical: Pulse sensor adjusts speed to heartbeat.
Overall: 50x50x50 cm organic form.
12/2025 – Buying Materials
Purchased for installation: servo motor, pulse sensor, turntable.
12/2025 – Exploring Möbius Strip Version
Switched to Möbius strip for endless loop animation.
Plant grows, dies, flips, repeats.
Heartbeat controls speed; light casts twisting shadows.
Tried lenticular for multi-angle motion.
Used real-time video from cameras or webcams.
Issue: Stacked strips can't rotate independently in Möbius form.
Solution: Cut slit, thread strips to interlock for rotation.
01/2026 – Lighting System Decision
Goal: Dynamic shadows that evoke cosmological time while staying electricity-free.
1. Switchable permanent magnets (no power, no heat, no noise).
2. Small servo motors slowly rotate magnet handles (strong → weak cycles).
3. Hanging wireless LED lamps with ferromagnetic bases respond to magnets.
4. Multiple lamps create evolving shadows like planets and moons.
Result: Pure poetic shadows with minimal hidden automation.
01/2026 – Barrier-Grid Animation Decisions
Frames: 8 total.
Content: Cosmic events (sunlight flash, children running, raindrops, sunsets).
Möbius strip feels like twisting galaxy – endless and shared.
Strip sizes: Inner 0.1 mm PVC (9×140 cm), outer acetate (10×140 cm).
Reasons for 8 frames:
Band width: exactly 175 mm per cycle.
Slit width: 22 mm (easy to print and align).
01/2026 – Spring-Powered Mechanism
Goal: Completely electricity-free movement.
Adopted music-box style spring (發條) to drive lamp swing.
Magnetic pendulum slows naturally; viewer winds spring to restart.
Symbol: Human effort keeps time flowing.
01/2026 – Animation Print Testing
Screen tests (iPad) showed 4-frame and 6-frame clear.
Printed on real PVC: effect disappeared due to ink opacity, thickness, and light scattering.
New tests planned:
• 4 frames × 7 pixels per stripe (~0.55 mm physical)
• 5 frames × 5 pixels per stripe (~0.42 mm physical)
Goal: Visible barrier-grid effect in physical print.
02/2026 – Major Concept Refinement
Removed projector and camera for simplicity and unity.
Decided to remove the projector and camera from the final setup. The projector was initially planned to project Processing-generated visual effects showing the shadow to the position of the audience as captured by the camera.
The change was made to simplify the work, make it more focused on the main content, and create a stronger sense of unity. The three aspects of perceiving time are now clearly expressed as:
1. Moving small metal lights = Cosmological perspective
2. People walking to view the barrier-grid animation = Philosophical perspective
3. Music box spring mechanism driven movement and music = Physical perspective
The film element will be presented in four different forms:
1. Unrolled film as a linear timeline
2. Ends-connected film as cyclical time
3. Möbius strip shaped film as unified and eternal time
4. Film in pieces (one photo per piece) to represent fragments of time
These film pieces will be hung or placed next to fixed spotlights on the wall. Viewers can choose a piece and project it onto a surface, either as a normal photo or as a barrier-grid animation.
