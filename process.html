GT Process Documentation: Ephemeral Cycles

Mid-August 2025 – Initial Brainstorming
Started exploring memento mori as the core concept.
First idea: a clock with barrier-grid animation.
Goal: gentle reminder of life’s transience using nature visuals.
Mid-September 2025 – First Advisor Meeting
Discussed initial idea.
Advisor suggested: do more research on “what is time?”
Key questions: What defines time? How can we interpret it in different ways?
End of September 2025 – Research Phase
1. Memento Mori
Origin: Latin phrase “remember you must die”. Used in art to encourage humility and mindfulness.

2. Perspectives on Time
PerspectiveCore Idea
PhysicalMeasurable, mechanical (clocks, seconds)
CosmologicalVast, cyclical (sun, seasons, stars)
PhilosophicalSubjective, experiential (memory, presence)
Early October 2025 – Personal Reflection
As a secondary school student, I felt time accelerate. By the time I noticed, those years were gone.
Big question: What does it mean to truly cherish time?
Temporal conclusion: Perhaps the way to cherish time is to ignore it entirely — focus only on the task, immerse in the experience, let time disappear.
Mid-October 2025 – Project Expansion
Decided to expand beyond one clock into a series:
1. Physical: Animated barrier-grid clock.
2. Cosmological: Sundial with subtle motion.
3. Philosophical: Non-functional “timepiece” that forces presence.

Early November 2025 – Animation Logic
Method: Split 518 frames into 129 groups (4 frames each) to create 129 interlaced strips.
Arranged side-by-side around a donut-shaped ring in Processing.
Unified Animation Strategy:
RingRotation SpeedPace
Inner1 turn / 60 secFast
Middle1 turn / 60 minModerate
Outer1 turn / 12 hrsSlow
Mid-November 2025 – Technical Development
Created a three-ring overlay using Adobe Illustrator with aligned grids.
Goal: Generate matching interlaced donut images that perfectly align.
End of November 2025 – Project Contraction
Abandoned the series for one unified interactive installation.
Includes Physical (cylinder), Cosmological (light/shadow), and Philosophical (pulse sensor) aspects.
Mid-December 2025 – Möbius Strip Version
The plant grows on one half, dies on the other, then flips and starts again forever.
Solution for rotation: Cut a slit and thread the second strip through to allow independent rotation.
Early January 2026 – Lighting System
Using switchable permanent magnets controlled by small servo motors.
Wireless LED lamps with ferromagnetic bases sway to changing magnetic fields.
Creates dynamic shadows simulating independent orbits of celestial bodies.
Mid-January 2026 – Animation Decisions
Chosen 8 frames for the Möbius strip animation.
Content: MTR sunlight, children running, raindrops, sunsets.
Heartbeat interaction: “My pulse controls the rhythm of the universe.”
End of January 2026 – Spring Mechanism
Adopted a spring-powered mechanism (發條) to achieve the physical aspect of time.
Electricity-free; viewer winds the spring to re-supply mechanical energy.
Symbolizes that time requires human effort to continue flowing.
Mid February 2026 – Concept Refinement
Removed projector and camera to focus on unity.
1. Moving lights = Cosmological
2. People walking = Philosophical
3. Spring mechanism = Physical
Early March 2026 – Construction Begins
Confirmed film content (36 frames). Cut into 9-frame loop and 27-frame Möbius strip.
Built the magnetic momentum mechanism and drilled the wooden base.
