Ephemeral Cycle

Wong Mei Po

Advisor: Professor LAM Miu Ling

Year

2026

Size

3×3×3m

Medium

Interactive Kinetic Installation

(incorporating mechanical film animation, music box spring mechanism, barrier-grid / lenticular film, directional lighting, and shadows)

Artist’s Statement

“Ephemeral Cycle” is a gentle, low-tech interactive installation that reinterprets the memento mori tradition as a quiet celebration of life and the preciousness of the present moment. Rather than evoking fear of death, the work invites viewers to slow down and cherish time as it unfolds. The sculpture features a central rotating film ring displaying a barrier-grid animation of a plant’s life cycle. A music box spring mechanism drives the rotation and produces soft music. A dim spotlight highlights the central ring, while torn sulfur paper pieces hang from the ceiling, creating a subtle mini-ruin atmosphere around the work

The installation expresses three perspectives of time: the mechanical movement of the music box represents the physical aspect; the directional light from above casting changing shadows through the abstract wooden trunks and branches represents the cosmological aspect; and the viewer’s own thoughts, memories, and imagination when looking at the barrier-grid animations and film pieces represent the philosophical aspect — reminding us that time is subjective and best valued by being fully present.

By using simple mechanical beauty and symbolic film forms, the work transforms the reminder of life’s transience into a poetic and hopeful experience. The main installation serves as the poetic focal point (Emptiness area), while a single TV placed on the side acts as secondary support material (Real Lived area), allowing viewers to clearly see the full animations. Together, they encourage reflection on the beauty of fleeting moments and the importance of cherishing the present.
